# Key Insights – Marketing & E-Commerce Analytics

This section summarizes the most important insights derived from SQL analysis and Power BI dashboards.

---

## 1. Funnel Performance Insights
- The largest user drop-off occurs between **Click → Add to Cart**, indicating friction in product selection or pricing.
- **Cart → Purchase conversion** is relatively strong, suggesting checkout flow is effective once intent is high.
- Bounce behavior highlights weak landing or product pages that fail to engage users.

---

## 2. Campaign & Traffic Insights
- A small number of campaigns contribute a disproportionately high share of total revenue (Pareto effect).
- Some traffic sources drive high user volume but low conversion, indicating low-quality traffic.
- Channels differ significantly in **AOV and conversion rate**, not just revenue volume.

---

## 3. Experiment (A/B Test) Insights
- The **Test group outperforms Control** in both conversion rate and revenue per user.
- Uplift is not uniform across devices, suggesting device-specific UX impact.
- Results indicate statistically meaningful improvement rather than random variation.

---

## 4. Customer & Product Insights
- Higher loyalty tiers generate significantly more **revenue per user**, validating retention strategies.
- Certain product categories rely heavily on discounts without proportional revenue uplift.
- Revenue concentration suggests focusing on **high-value customers** rather than pure acquisition.

---

## 5. Business Implications
- Improve mid-funnel experience to reduce drop-offs.
- Reallocate marketing spend toward high-converting channels.
- Scale winning experiment variants.
- Optimize discount strategy at category level.
